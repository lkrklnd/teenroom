Materials

Things you can borrow. Things you can use.

Multimedia

Borrow a selection of books, magazines, video games, ebooks, audiobooks, comics, and manga chosen specifically with teens in mind.

Games

Services

Things we can help you with. Things we can direct you to.

Guidance

Need a recommendation? Want to learn something new? Have a problem you need help solving? We can connect you to the support and resources you need.

Homework Help

Get help with schoolwork from librarians daily or on a first-come first-served basis from Brandeis student volunteers every Monday and Wednesday from 4-6.

Job Skills

We can guide you through the search, application, and interview process. Ask to use us as a reference. Take a personality test to explore careers suited to you.

Volunteer

Earn work experience by volunteering. Help with projects. Organize an event. Start and lead a club. Help promote the library. Write for our Instagram.

Life Prep

Study and practice for PSAT, ACT, SAT, or AP tests; find colleges and scholarships; and explore careers through Peterson's Testing and Education Center.

Driver's Ed

Want to get your driver's license? Find out how; download the manual; and take a variety of practice tests with our online driver's education resources.

Learn a Language

Learn English, Spanish, Haitian Creole, Mandarin, and many more through fun, interactive lessons on a desktop, phone, or tablet with Mango Languages.

Other Resources

Find legal help, check Consumer Resources product reviews, explore your ancestry, download music, and more for free through our online resources.

Magic Mondays

Something's happening at the Library every Monday at 6

Stop by the Teen Room every Monday at 6 for our weekly Teen Room programming. Twice a month, we'll have Real Talk, a youth-led forum for discussing the issues that are most important to teens. Once a month, we'll host interesting professionals at our career series, Journey of Becoming (J.O.B.). Remaining Mondays are up to you! Minute-to-Win-It. Yoga. Movie nights. Pumpkin carving. Real Talk

Real conversations about the things that really matter.

Real Talk is a youth-led forum for discussing the issues that are most important to teens. We meet two Mondays a month from 6-8 in the Waltham Public Library Teen Room. Each event is planned, promoted, and executed by teen leaders with help from our Teen Specialist. In exchange for their work, our teen leaders receive training, support, work history, and a stipend. Real Talk is made possible through the generosity of the Library Initiative for Teens and Tweens (LITT), a project of the Rhyme and Reason Fund at the Boston Foundation.

Journey of Becoming (J.O.B.)

A career exploration series for future professionals.

Interested in a particular career but don't quite know how to get there? Not sure how you might turn your interests into a reliable paycheck? Stop by Journey of Becoming (J.O.B.)! Once a month we invite professionals from a variety of fields to explain what they do for work and how they came to find their careers. We've hosted biologist and TV show host Pat Spain, children's book author and DJ Jef Czekaj, research assistants from Boston Children's Hospital, Lasell college athletes, rap historian Brian Coleman, and many more. They share their upbringings, their education, their missteps and mistakes, as well as their successes and guidance. As much as anything, we think finding your career is a process of self-discovery. Stop by to see what you might learn about yourself!

Girls Who Code

Learn to code in a fun and supportive environment

Our Girls Who Code club teaches girls 12-18 with any level of coding experience how coding skills can help them pursue their passion, whatever it is. Our experienced instructors help girls explore storytelling, illustration, gaming, music production, and more through a variety of coding technologies. But it's always more club than class!

Bring your own computer or use one of ours. Meet visiting women working in tech careers. And once a year we go on a field trip to someplace amazing like the Facebook offices in Cambridge or the Harmonix offices in Boston (makers of Rock Band!).

The Club runs from October to May. Information sessions and registration occur in September.

Want to know more? Email our Teen Specialist.
